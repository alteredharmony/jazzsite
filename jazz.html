Overview

In my understanding of music there are two approaches to learning it.

  1. With sheet music
  2. By ear
These are not mutually exclusive approaches to learning but how you approach each one will completely change your relationship to music. Historically jazz has been learned by ear. Jazz musicians in the days that jazz formed listened to each other and learned from listening to other jazz musicians. I would argue that in jazz your ear is your main instrument. Getting your ear to connect to your instrument is your primary technique in jazz. Learn jazz language, play jazz language, hear jazz language.

All this is not to say that there is no use in learning to read music or learning a jazz song by reading. It is merely to say that hearing jazz in your mind is very important. While learning some jazz language by reading may be helpful, it is also important to listen closely to jazz and be able to pick up the infelctions of it. An analogy would be to learing a language. If you only learned to read a languiage, that would not be sufficient to be able to speak it.

With this in mind here are some things to do to learn jazz language.

  1. Listen to jazz passively
  2. Listen to jazz actively
  3. Learn jazz songs
  4. Learn jazz licks
  5. Learn jazz solos
  6. Learn jazz rhythms
  7. Learn at least some songs, licks and solos by ear

Another important skill to have is to be able to play your instrument fluently. When you hear jazz language in your head, you want to be able to easily translate it onto your instruement. Some of the skills to do this will be specific to your instrument and others will apply to many or all instruments.

My primary instruement is the guitar, so the following examples are examples of skills to have for the guitar. If you don't play guitar then you could brainstorm or research skills that you must have to play your instrument. If you do play guitar then you may brainstorm or research other skills that you may want to know for your instrument.
